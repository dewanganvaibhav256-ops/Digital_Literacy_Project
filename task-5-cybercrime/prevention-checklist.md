# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: Student Prevention Checklist

Navigating campus life in India requires staying digitally sharp. Use this checklist to secure your devices, data, and finances.

### 🔐 Account & Device Security
- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on your university portals, Gmail, and social media. Use an authenticator app rather than just SMS.
- [ ] **Audit App Permissions:** Review which apps have access to your camera, microphone, and contacts. Delete any "free" study tools or photo editors you no longer use.
- [ ] **Secure Campus Wi-Fi Usage:** Always use a trusted VPN when connecting to university or cafe Wi-Fi to prevent data sniffing.
- [ ] **Instant Software Updates:** Install security patches for your OS and apps immediately; hackers often exploit known vulnerabilities in older versions.
- [ ] **Set Social Profiles to Private:** Prevent "identity cloning" by restricting who can see your photos and personal details, reducing the risk of scammers impersonating you to borrow money from friends.
- [ ] **Scrutinize "Urgent" Alerts:** Treat any message about "expired scholarships" or "overdue library fines" as suspicious. Verify directly with the department before clicking links.

### 💸 Financial & UPI Safety
- [ ] **PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving:** Never enter your UPI PIN to receive a refund, prize, or payment. A PIN is **only** required to deduct money from your account.
- [ ] **Verify QR Code Merchant Names:** When scanning a QR code at a canteen or shop, always confirm the name displayed on the screen matches the actual merchant before authorizing payment.

---

### 🚨 Reporting Channels
If you encounter a cybercrime or financial fraud, report it immediately to the authorities:

* **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Call immediately for financial fraud to initiate the "Golden Hour" fund freeze).

# Social Media Responsibility Checklist

As a college student, your digital footprint is often the first thing recruiters or graduate schools see. Use this checklist to ensure your online presence remains a professional asset.

---

## ✅ The "Do's"
- [ ] **Audit your privacy settings regularly.** Ensure you know exactly who can see your personal posts on every platform.
- [ ] **Network with intent.** Follow industry leaders and professional groups to build a career-oriented circle.
- [ ] **Fact-check before sharing.** Verify the credibility of news or academic information to avoid spreading misinformation.
- [ ] **Highlight your achievements.** Share your projects, volunteer work, and certifications to show growth and competence.
- [ ] **Support and uplift your peers.** Use your platform to congratulate others and foster a positive community.

## ❌ The "Don'ts"
- [ ] **Don't post in the heat of the moment.** Avoid "venting" or posting while angry; emotional posts often lead to later regrets.
- [ ] **Don't share sensitive personal information.** Never post your real-time location, home address, or student ID number.
- [ ] **Don't engage with "trolls" or cyberbullies.** Public digital arguments can reflect poorly on your conflict-resolution skills.
- [ ] **Don't post content depicting policy-breaking acts.** This includes anything that violates your university’s code of conduct.
- [ ] **Don't plagiarize or ignore copyright.** Always give credit when sharing someone else's work, whether it is art, code, or writing.

---

> **The Billboard Test:** Before you hit "Post," ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable seeing this on a giant billboard in the middle of campus with my name attached to it?"
